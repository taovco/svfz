The artist discusses his love for the many eccentric creatures that can be found on New York City’s sidewalks.



“Beasts and Beauties”

New York City has long been a beacon for nonconformists of all stripes. Its streets teem with life and offer many excellent opportunities for people- or monster-watching, as depicted on this week’s cover by the artist Edward Steed.

You never know who, or what, you’ll see on the streets in New York. Do you enjoy walking around and observing the scene?

Yes. In the cover illustration, I was trying to depict the varieties of monsters we have here in New York—the city attracts a completely different crowd than the British countryside, where I grew up.



You started making little clay figurines during quarantine. How did that come about?

I like making things with clay, but didn’t do it much. It takes longer and is harder to get right, but I had more time during lockdown. I find that a good figurine is more satisfying than a good drawing—closer to being alive, and more interesting to share a home with.

Do you find it easier to come up with new characters when you are drawing or when you are working with clay figurines? Or is there something to be found in moving back and forth between the two?

Every medium forces you to think in a certain way. Some shapes are hard to draw but easy to sculpt, and vice versa, so it can be useful to switch between two. I just keep adding to a drawing or sculpture until it seems alive to me.



I’d like to make a film next.

If the creatures talked, would they all speak the same language? If not, how would they communicate?

They all know at least a bit of American English, some more than others. But they all manage to get their point across.


This cast of characters feels appropriate, as we are just weeks out from Halloween. Did you grow up with the holiday, and how do you feel about it now? Do you dress up?

I have never celebrated it in any way.
