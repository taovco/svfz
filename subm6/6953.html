Director of Dutch wish-granting organization accused of having sex with underage boys


Peter V., the former director of Kanjer Wens Nederland, is suspected of having sex with underage boys during work for the foundation that grants the wishes of severely ill children. The 54-year-old man from Almere worked at the foundation until October last year, the Public Prosecution Service (OM) confirmed in a statement after questions
from Omroep Flevoland.

The man is suspected of sexually abusing two underage boys in January 2020. While investigating these charges,
the authorities found indications that V. may also have been involved in another sex crime with a third underage
boy
that happened before he worked for Kanjer Wens Nederland. The Chamber of Commerce confirmed that the
54-year-old suspect is no longer the foundation director on paper. But sources said that V. is still at the office on Bolderweg in Almere daily, drives a lease car, and still makes decisions for the foundation. according to Omroep Flevoland's sources, V. was still "well regarded" and remained active for the foundation. That led to six members
of the Supervisory Board resigning in October last year, the sources said.


"When the investigation has been completed, the OM will draw up a final indictment," .

V. was arrested in June last year. On June 24, the court released him from pre-trial detention on the condition
that he is not allowed to work with children. He was again arraigned on Wednesday. The magistrate rejected the
OM's request to put V. back in custody.

Omroep Flevoland approached Kanjer Wens Nederland for a response, but the foundation did not want to comment.

Update: An earlier version of this article included the headline, "Director of Dutch make a wish foundation accused
of having sex with underage boys." We did not intend to implicate the Dutch organization, Make-A-Wish Nederland, or the American organization, Make-A-Wish Foundation. We modified the headline to reflect that, and regret the confusion.
