27 possible voter fraud cases in 3 million Wisconsin ballots


Wisconsin election officials identified 27 potential cases of voter fraud out of 3.3 million ballots cast and forwarded them to local district attorneys.

More than half of the cases came in a single city, where 16 people had registered with their mailing address at a UPS store.
No charges have yet been filed. Also, future cases of potential fraud could always be forwarded to prosecutors.


The identified potential cases of fraud are in line with suspected voter misconduct in past elections.
Wisconsin Republican lawmakers are pushing more than a dozen bills this year that would make it more difficult to vote absentee in Wisconsin and any that pass are expected to be vetoed by Democratic Gov. Tony Evers.

The bills are part of a national push by Republicans to change voting laws following the Nov election.

Republicans in Wi have also ordered an audit of the election, which is expected to take months, and the Assembly Elections Committee has approved launching its own investigation. Lawmakers say they received thousands of complaints of irregularities,
but the records obtained show just a handful that were identified by election officials.



The AP received every case identified by clerks that were forwarded. The records detail cases where election officials noted an irregularity that may be have been illegal and flagged it for prosecutors to investigate.

More than half of the total cases, were people in La Crosse whose registered address was at a UPS Store.

Of the 11 other cases: four people voted both in person and absentee; one was a convicted felon; one was an absentee ballot returned by the son of the person who requested it and who voted in person; three people voted in two places; one person returned two absentee ballots; and one person who had been adjudicated incompetent and wasn’t allowed to cast a ballot.
Republican legislative leaders did not immediately return messages.

“Whatever excuse the Republicans have for massive fraud in Wisconsin, it’s just simply not true,” Erpenbach said.

The Wisconsin Elections Commission will be conducting audits for voting by felons and cross-state voting as soon as data becomes available, said the agency’s spokesman Reid Magney. Those numbers will be reported later this year, he said.

The last report, found just 15 cases of suspected fraud. The latest numbers are in line with past elections, Magney said.
