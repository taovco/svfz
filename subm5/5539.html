Berlin: Despite the onset of winter, the left votes to evict unvaccinated homeless people from the underground platforms

Despite the winter, the Berlin left has decided that there can be no exceptions to the rule: the homeless who used to take refuge in the underground (ER: metro) to keep warm will no longer be able to do so unless they are vaccinated, cured of COVID or tested.
COVID-19 has definitely shattered the fragile veneer of humanity that characterises our civilisation.



It was one of several headlines in the world press on Wednesday 8 December: the Berlin Senate has introduced the so-called 3G system (“vaccinated, cured or tested”) in the city’s underground. It will no longer be possible to stand on a platform without a health pass.

When questioned, the senators said they regretted that this would also affect the poorest people, those who have no roof over their heads and who, as winter approaches, take refuge in the metro. It is not possible to make an exception.

The fact that the city’s social services explain that they are taking the situation very seriously and will do everything possible to ensure that the “homeless” can benefit from reception areas and be vaccinated does not change the reality: a government alliance comprising the Social Democrats, the Greens and the Left is expelling the poorest from one of the few places where they could find shelter.

For the record, nearly 19% of Germans live below the poverty line, set at 1,074 euros by the country’s statistical office.

Berlin is trying 'help convince' the homeless to get vaccinated.

https://eturbonews.com/3011098/new-covi ... -the-cold/
Effective today, those without health certificates will be barred from entering the platforms, while security staff and ‘specially trained inspectors’ will conduct checks and enforce the regulations, Berlin authorities said in a statement on the city website.

All ‘other parts’ of the train stations will still be accessible to anyone, they added.

The measures will not just affect passengers, but the homeless as well, since many of them use metro stations and railway platforms to keep themselves warm during the winter. The city social services department acknowledged the issue but said that ‘for the reasons of infection protection, an exception is not desired’ and thus is not ‘possible’ for the homeless.

Instead, the Berlin authorities plan to ramp up efforts aimed at making vaccination and testing more accessible for the homeless, to help them comply with the regulations. The social services department operates free public testing sites, enabling the homeless to be tested on a daily basis.

The city also said that a ‘daytime meeting place’ for around 200 homeless people will soon be open at a large restaurant in the city center known as the Hofbrauhaus. “A good alternative, including a testing option, will be created for all those who would otherwise only have access to the train stations,” a social services official told local media.

Berlin also currently offers around 1,150 places in overnight shelters for the homeless.
